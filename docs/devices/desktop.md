# Plane desktop app

The Plane desktop app brings your full workspace to a native app on macOS, Windows, and Linux. Manage work items, plan with cycles and modules, write pages, triage your Inbox, and chat with Plane AI — all in a dedicated window, separate from your browser.

::: info
The desktop app currently works with Plane Cloud only. Self-hosted instances don't support the desktop app yet — support is coming in an upcoming release.
:::

## Sign in

Open the app and sign in with your existing Plane Cloud account — there's no separate desktop account. Sign-in opens in your browser, where you continue with your email and a magic code, your password, or an SSO provider such as Google, GitHub, or your company's single sign-on. You return to the app once you are signed in.

::: tip
You need a Plane account to sign in. If you do not have a workspace yet, you can create one or accept an invite first.
:::

## Tabbed navigation

Open multiple projects, views, and pages in tabs within a single window, and switch between them without losing your place — much like tabs in a browser. Keep the work you care about a click away instead of navigating back and forth.

## Notifications

The app shows a native badge on your dock or taskbar with your unread Inbox count, so you know when something needs attention without opening the window.

::: info
OS-level push notifications are not currently supported on the desktop app.
:::

## Updates

The desktop app keeps itself up to date automatically, so you always have the latest version without re-downloading.
